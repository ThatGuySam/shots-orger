Looking at this directory structure, I can see a **Screenshots** folder that has evolved over time with different organizational approaches:

## Overall Structure

The directory contains screenshots and screen recordings spanning from **2020 to now**, organized in three distinct patterns:

### 1. **Archived Years (2020-2023): Year → Month Structure**
```
_2020/
  ├── 01 Jan/
  ├── 02 Feb/
  ├── 03 March/
  └── ...
_2021/
_2022/
_2023/
```
- Years prefixed with underscores (`_2020`, `_2021`, etc.)
- Monthly sub-folders within each year
- Month names spelled out ("01 Jan", "02 Feb", "03 March", etc.)

### 2. **Current Year (2024): Monthly Folders at Root Level**
```
01 Jan/
02 Feb/
03 Mar/
04 Apr/
05 May/
06 Jun/
```
- Monthly folders directly in the root directory
- No year-level wrapper folder
- Same naming convention as archived years

### 3. **Recent Files (2024-2025): Loose Files at Root**
- Unorganized screenshots and recordings scattered at the root level
- Mix of naming conventions:
  - Standard: `Screenshot 2025-MM-DD at H.MM.SS AM.png`
  - Timestamp format: `2025-MM-DD_HH-MM-SS.mov`

## File Types & Content
- **Screenshots** (.png): Primarily macOS screenshots and iOS Simulator captures
- **Screen Recordings** (.mov): Video captures of screen activity
- **Misc Files**: Some .mp3, .jpeg, .gif files

## Evolution Pattern
The organization suggests this system evolved over time:
1. **2020-2023**: Systematic year/month archival structure
2. **2024**: Transition period with monthly folders at root
3. **2025**: More casual approach with loose files

This appears to be a personal screenshot collection that started very organized but became less structured over time, possibly as the volume of files increased or usage patterns changed.
